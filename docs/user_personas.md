# MoneyOS User Personas

These personas define who the current app is built for and how the product framing should adapt.

The core answer stays the same for everyone:

- `What is safe right now?`
- `What should I protect first?`

## 1. Salaried User

### Profile

- fixed monthly salary
- uses a mix of cash, UPI, card, and bank
- wants one clear answer before next salary

### Pain Points

- bank balance feels safer than reality
- forgets bills, EMI, subscriptions, or school fees
- does not know what is truly free before next salary

### What Success Looks Like

- understands what is safe till next salary
- sees dues clearly
- can correct reality with one or two taps

## 2. Daily Wage Worker

### Profile

- earns daily or weekly
- often cash-first
- money is spent quickly across small needs

### Pain Points

- monthly framing feels unnatural
- cash leaks are easy to miss
- long forms and finance language are barriers

### What Success Looks Like

- can update money quickly
- knows what is still safe today
- sees whether things are getting tight

## 3. Farmer / Seasonal Earner

### Profile

- income comes in uneven waves
- home money and work money may mix
- cash and informal obligations are common

### Pain Points

- monthly budget logic does not fit reality
- hard to plan between money events
- obligations may live in memory, not in tools

### What Success Looks Like

- sees what must be protected before the next earning cycle
- gets a practical answer without a monthly-only framing

## 4. Business / Self-Employed User

### Profile

- runs a shop, service, trade, or gig-based work
- home and business money may mix
- cares more about usable cashflow than formal accounting

### Pain Points

- too many tools assume accounting behavior
- dues and cash movement are hard to track simply
- free money is hard to separate from business churn

### What Success Looks Like

- understands what is truly free after obligations
- can keep reality updated without bookkeeping discipline

## 5. Family Money Manager

### Profile

- may or may not be the main earner
- manages groceries, fees, medicine, rent, and household cash

### Pain Points

- needs one household answer, not many screens
- may not trust finance apps that feel technical or judgmental
- juggles dues mentally

### What Success Looks Like

- sees what is safe for the household
- knows what still needs to be kept aside
- can act without overthinking the app
